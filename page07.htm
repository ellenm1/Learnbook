TB Overview

Background on Tuberculosis

Tuberculosis (TB) is a disease caused by the bacterium, Mycobacterium tuberculosis. In humans it usually causes a debilitating lung infection or pneumonia that requires treatment with special antibiotic medications. If a person with TB disease does not receive appropriate treatment, it can lead to severe illness and death.

TB was once the leading cause of death in the United States. With the discovery of anti-tuberculosis drugs beginning in 1945, the incidence of TB in the US dropped sharply. From 2004-2008, 10 or fewer patients with infectious TB were identified per year at the University of Michigan Hospitals and Health Centers (UMHHC) and also in Washtenaw County.
