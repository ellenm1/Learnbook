Central Line-Associated Bloodstream Infection Prevention

Approximately 250,000 bloodstream infections (BSIs) occur each year in the United States (1) and approximately 90% are associated with central venous catheters (CVCs) (2).

Central line-associated bloodstream infection (CLABSIs) increase morbidity, mortality (up to 35%), length of hospitalization (by a mean of 7 days), and costs (up to $28,000).

CVCs disrupt the integrity of the skin making infection with microorganisms (primarily bacteria and fungi) possible.  Infection may then spread to the bloodstream (bacteremia) and hemodynamic changes and organ dysfunction (sepsis) may ensue.
