Standard Precautions

Definition:Â 
A group of infection prevention practices that apply to all patients, regardless of diagnosis or presumed infection status. Standard Precautions is based on the principle that all blood, body fluids, secretions, excretions (except sweat), non-intact skin, and mucous membranes may contain transmissible infectious agents.

Principles:
Standard Precautions includes the use of personal protective equipment (PPE), hand hygiene, cleaning practices, respiratory hygiene, and safe sharps practices.

Practices:
Touching mucous membranes, non intact skin, and body substances require the use of PPE. In addition, equipment or items in the patient environment likely to have been contaminated with blood or body fluids must be handled in a manner to prevent transmission of infectious agents.

These practices include:

  • Wear gloves for handling heavily soiled equipment
  • Properly clean and disinfect or sterilize reusable equipment before use on another patient
